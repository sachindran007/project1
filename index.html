Plot Overview

Plot Overview of The Proposal by Anton Chekhov

Anton Chekhov’s The Proposal is a one-act farcical comedy that humorously portrays human vanity, pride, and the absurdity of arguments over trivial matters, especially in the context of marriage.

Introduction (Exposition)

The play is set in the rural countryside of Russia. Ivan Lomov, a wealthy but nervous landowner, arrives at the house of Stepan Chubukov, his neighbor. Chubukov, a cunning yet friendly old man, is delighted to see him and welcomes him warmly. Lomov, dressed formally, appears agitated and anxious, which makes Chubukov curious about his visit. Lomov soon reveals that he has come to propose marriage to Natalya Stepanovna, Chubukov’s 25-year-old daughter. Chubukov, excited by the prospect of a wealthy match for his daughter, gives his enthusiastic approval and rushes to call Natalya.

Rising Action: The Argument Over Oxen Meadows

When Natalya enters, Lomov tries to express his feelings, but due to his nervousness, he gets sidetracked and mentions a disputed piece of land called Oxen Meadows. He claims the land belongs to him, but Natalya strongly disagrees, insisting that it is her family's property. What starts as a polite discussion soon escalates into a heated argument. Both accuse each other's ancestors of illegally claiming the land. Chubukov joins in, siding with his daughter, and the argument intensifies into a shouting match. In the midst of the chaos, Lomov, who suffers from heart palpitations and nervous disorders, becomes extremely agitated and collapses from stress.

Climax: The Dispute Over Dogs

Natalya, unaware of Lomov’s intention to propose, continues to argue fiercely. When Chubukov finally lets slip that Lomov had actually come to propose, Natalya panics and immediately regrets the argument. She insists on calling Lomov back and agrees with whatever he says. However, the peace does not last long. Lomov and Natalya start arguing again—this time about whose dog, Squeezer (Natalya’s) or Guess (Lomov’s), is better. Chubukov gets involved yet again, and the fight becomes even worse than before. Lomov collapses again, this time seemingly unconscious. Natalya and Chubukov, thinking he has died, panic.

Falling Action: The Proposal Finally Happens

After a few tense moments, Lomov regains consciousness. Chubukov, desperate to settle the matter, forces Lomov and Natalya to accept the marriage proposal without any more arguments.

Conclusion: A Comically Uncertain Ending

Even after their engagement, Natalya and Lomov continue arguing, showing that their marriage will likely be filled with similar disputes. Chubukov, exhausted and frustrated, sarcastically blesses them and asks for champagne to celebrate, though he seems more relieved than happy.

Themes in The Proposal

  1. Marriage as a Social Institution – The play humorously critiques how marriage is often based on financial and social considerations rather than love.
  2. Absurdity of Human Nature – The play highlights how people fight over trivial matters while ignoring what is truly important.
  3. Ego and Pride – The characters’ stubbornness and need to "win" arguments overshadow their original intentions.

Final Thoughts

Despite being a short one-act play, The Proposal is a masterpiece of comic drama, filled with irony, exaggerated conflicts, and satirical social commentary. The play ultimately shows that human beings often prioritize their pride over relationships, even in something as significant as marriage.

About the Author

Anton Chekhov, a Russian playwright and short-story writer, is known for his masterful character-driven narratives and exploration of human nature.
